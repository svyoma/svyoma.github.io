Similar Utterance in Saṃskṛta and Prākṛta works - 1

Only one among hundred is valiant!

शतेषु जायते शूरः सहस्रेषु च पण्डितः।
वक्ता दशसहस्रेषु दाता भवति वा न वा॥

Most of us are well-acquainted with this very popular verse. While searching for its source on wikisource, it shows Skandapurāṇa as one of the prominent reference [pāda 4 - दाता जायेत वा न वा].
The source of Saṃskṛta verse striked me as, today, I came across same utterance in a different tongue. A Prākṛta version of this verse occurs in a work called Vasudevahiṇḍi.

सतेसु जायते सूरो सहस्सेसु य पंडिओ।
वत्ता सयसहस्सेसु दाया जायति वा ण वा॥
इंदियाणं जए सूरो धम्मं चरति पंडिओ।
वत्ता सच्चवओ होइ दाया भूयहिए रओ॥

[शतेषु जायते शूरः सहस्रेषु च पण्डितः।
वक्ता शतसहस्रेषु दाता भवति वा न वा॥
इन्द्रियाणां जये शूरो धर्मं चरति पण्डितः।
वक्ता सत्यव्रतो भवति दाता भूतहिते रतः॥]

- कहामुहं १-२

"One among the hundred becomes a valiant. Only one among a thousand becomes a person learned. One among a thousand becomes an orator. A donor might be born or not.
One becomes valiant by victory over senses. One who practices Dharma is learned. An orator is one adheres truth. A donor is one who is engaged in welfare of beings."

Vasudevahiṇḍi is regarded as closest edition and Jaina abridgement to Bṛhatkathā of Guṇāḍhya. It is authored in 5th century by Saṅghadāsagaṇī.
